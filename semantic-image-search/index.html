Overall, SIS has transformed the company’s workflow and increased productivity across the company. The head of design and creative who adopted the solution has received great feedback from teams across the organisation, who appreciate the ease of use and time-saving benefits of the system.

The AI Tech behind SIS

SIS AI works similarly to how a librarian organizes books in a library. Just as a librarian uses their knowledge and experience to categorize books based on their content, SIS AI uses a powerful tool called Image Language Interpreter (ILI): A cutting-edge AI system that uses advanced language models to interpret and understand the content of images, to organize images based on their content. Image Language Interpreter is like a librarian who can “read” images and understand what is depicted in them.

When a new image is added to the system, SIS AI uses Image Language Interpreter to “read” the image and assign relevant tags to it based on its content. This allows the image to be easily found and retrieved later using natural language search terms.

For example, if you are looking for an image of a beach, you can simply type “beach” into the search bar, and SIS AI will use Image Language Interpreter to find all images that have been tagged with the keyword “beach”. It’s like having a librarian who knows exactly where every book is located in the library, without having to manually search through shelves.

Training ILI, an AI system that can understand images and text together, is a lot like teaching a child. Just like a child needs to learn from many different sources to understand the world around them, ILI needs to learn from a huge variety of images and their associated text descriptions.

For example, just as a child might learn that a furry, four-legged animal that barks is called a dog after seeing many different pictures and hearing people say the word “dog,” ILI also learns to associate images of dogs with the word “dog” through exposure to many examples. However, instead of relying on a single teacher or parent, ILI learns from a vast collection of images and their associated text, allowing it to recognize and understand an incredible range of objects and concepts.

Overall, SIS AI’s use of ILI helps to streamline the process of organizing and retrieving images, making it faster and easier for users to find what they need.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to Common Questions about SIS: Streamlining Your Image Management

What is SIS and how can it benefit my company?

SIS stands for Semantic Image Search, and it is an AI-powered extension for your existing CMS that allows you to quickly search and find images based on their content. This saves your team time and money by reducing the need for manual tagging and sorting, and makes it easier to reuse existing images across your organization.

How does SIS compare to other image search tools?

SIS uses cutting-edge AI technology, including patented ILI, to provide accurate and relevant search results based on the content of images. This is a major improvement over traditional keyword-based search tools, which often require manual tagging and can be limited by language barriers or differences in terminology.

Can SIS be integrated with my existing CMS?

Yes, SIS is designed to work seamlessly with a variety of popular CMS systems, including WordPress and Drupal. Our team can help you set up and configure the extension to work with your specific platform and workflows.

How does SIS handle privacy and security concerns?

SIS is designed with security and privacy in mind, and we take great care to protect your data and ensure compliance with relevant regulations. All images and data are stored securely on your own servers, and our extension does not collect any personally identifiable information.

How does SIS handle duplicates or near-duplicates in our image library?

SIS uses advanced algorithms to identify and remove duplicates or near-duplicates in your image library. When new images are added to the system, SIS automatically checks for similar images and groups them together for easy management. This ensures that your image library remains organized and clutter-free.
